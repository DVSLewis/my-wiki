Why ETHis Matters
by Donny Lewis
The Deutsches Museum in Munich was built to hold the machines that changed everything. Turbines. Aircraft. The instruments of the industrial age, all under one roof, so that ordinary people could walk past what had once seemed impossible and feel, maybe for the first time, that the impossible was something humans actually do. That was the idea in 1903. That has always been the idea.
ETHis happens there in July. That is not a coincidence.
We are not gathering because Ethereum needs another conference. We are gathering because the hardest thing in any technological moment is not the building — it is the remembering of why we started. The noise gets loud. The prices move. The narratives shift. And somewhere in that movement, the original question gets buried: what are we actually building this for?
ETHis asks that question in a building full of answers to harder ones.
Public goods. Regenerative finance. Coordination at scale without the coercion that has historically made coordination possible. These are not small ambitions. Civilizations have collapsed trying to solve them with less capable tools. We have the tools now. What we need is the clarity.
I believe we find clarity in community. Not the online kind, which is fast and useful and often hollow — but in rooms where people look at each other and commit. ETHis is one of those rooms.
The machines in the Deutsches Museum were built by people who did not know they were making history. They were just solving the problem in front of them.
We should probably do the same.
